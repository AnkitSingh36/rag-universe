Interactive Assessment
🧩 Does Your Project Need RAG?

5 questions to diagnose your use case. Each "Yes" answer adds to your RAG score.

Question 1
Does your AI need to answer questions about YOUR specific documents or data?
Yes — it needs to know about MY files, database, or documents (+2 points)
No — general knowledge is fine
Question 2
Does the information your AI needs change over time?
Yes — information gets updated (news, prices, policies…) (+2 points)
No — the facts are mostly stable and permanent
Question 3
How serious are hallucinations (the AI making up false facts)?
Very serious — wrong answers could cause harm or legal issues (+2 points)
Not critical — a few wrong answers are acceptable
Question 4
What kind of questions will users ask?
Specific factual questions about documents ("What is the refund policy?") (+1 point)
General / creative questions ("Write me a poem about nature")
Question 5
Do you have documents / data you want the AI to reference?
Yes — I have PDFs, CSVs, wikis, databases, etc. (+1 point)
No — I just want AI to answer from its training data

Real-World Scenarios
📋 9 Use Cases — Should You Use RAG?

Learn from real examples across different industries and project types.

✅ Use RAG
Company Knowledge Base
AI answers questions about internal policies, HR docs, and wikis. Data changes monthly — RAG is perfect.
✅ Use RAG
Customer Support Bot
Answers from product manuals and FAQs. Users ask specific questions that need verified facts, not guesses.
✅ Use RAG
Legal Document Q&A
Lawyers query contracts and case files. Hallucinations are unacceptable — RAG grounds answers in source text.
✅ Use RAG
Medical Records Assistant
Queries patient records or research papers. Accuracy is critical — RAG retrieves exact passages.
⚡ Maybe
News Summarizer
Depends on freshness needs. If you feed new articles daily, RAG works. If summaries are about general trends, prompting may suffice.
⚡ Maybe
Coding Assistant
RAG helps for proprietary codebases. For general coding questions, a fine-tuned model or large-context LLM may work better.
❌ Skip RAG
Creative Writing Helper
No documents to retrieve from — the AI generates from imagination. Prompting or fine-tuning is the right approach.
❌ Skip RAG
Language Translation
Translation is a generation task, not retrieval. LLMs already do this well without needing document lookup.
❌ Skip RAG
General Chatbot (no docs)
If you just want small talk or trivia, the LLM's training data is enough. RAG adds complexity with no benefit.
Decision Framework
🗺️ RAG vs Alternatives

Know when to reach for each tool in your AI toolbox.

Approach Best When Limitation Complexity
🔍 RAG Private docs, fresh data, factual Q&A Needs a document corpus Medium
✏️ Fine-tuning Stable domain knowledge, style adaptation Expensive, data goes stale High
💬 Prompting Small docs fit in context window Token limits, cost at scale Low
🏗️ Base LLM only General knowledge, creative tasks Hallucinations, no private data Low
